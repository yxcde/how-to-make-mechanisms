How to Make Mechanisms of Control

UdK Berlin / Summer Semester 2026

Course description

Art no longer just hangs on a wall; it breathes, moves, and reacts. To truly understand the hidden systems behind interactive art, we must build them. A hands-on, rapid-prototyping studio course focused on bridging digital intention with physical reality. Every week, discover a new technology. Work in small collectives (2-3) to build and present a functional, conceptual prototype over the next seven days. The Goal: Think through making. We favour experimentation and iteration over perfection.

Below you’ll find the slide decks, demo codes, additional material, and references of our weekly sessions. All content is for study use only.

Week 02 — From Electrons to Expression

Basics

We talked about how electrons move, what voltage and current mean physically, why resistance matters, and how microcontrollers “see” analog vs. digital signals. We also looked at how this translates into artistic control (light, motion, potentially sound).
